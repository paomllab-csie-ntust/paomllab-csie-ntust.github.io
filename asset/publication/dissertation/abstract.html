Doctoral Dissertation Abstract

Title

A Continuous Model for Salient Shape Selection and Representation

Author

Hsing-Kuo Kenneth Pao

Institution

New York University

Date

April 2001

Abstract

This dissertation presents a continuous model for salient shape selection and representation in computer vision. The work focuses on developing computational methods for identifying and representing perceptually significant shapes in visual scenes, with applications to figure/ground separation, shape analysis, and pattern recognition.

The research explores how continuous mathematical models can be used to capture the essential characteristics of shapes that are salient to human perception, providing a foundation for automated visual analysis systems.
